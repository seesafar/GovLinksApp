SECTION 01 UDSAL Concept

What is OneLink UDSAL™?

Unified Digital Services Access Layer · A unified architectural layer for digital services.

OneLink UDSAL™ is an architectural layer that provides unified access to government and global digital services via a single data schema (Unified Service Schema), a global deep search engine, and multi-region routing that lives entirely on the front-end.

UDSAL aims to make access to digital services simpler, clearer, and more reliable, so that end users do not need to know every portal or site; they only interact with a single unified layer.

From an engineering perspective, UDSAL acts as an abstraction layer above services, normalizing how they are described, discovered, and linked across multiple countries and platforms.

SECTION 03 Logo Philosophy

UDSAL Logo & Usage

Dual Crescent · Core Orb · Hybrid Glow · A symbol of a unified core connecting the digital world.

The logo is built around a Core Orb representing the UDSAL engine, surrounded by a dual crescent that encodes multiple countries and services orbiting a single access layer.

The hybrid green gradient expresses the transition from local to global and from government services to international platforms, matching OneLink’s vision.

Do: • Use the logo on UDSAL-related pages and dashboards.
• Keep the official colors and glow intensity.
• Place it on dark, calm backgrounds for maximum legibility.
Don’t: • Replace government or institutional logos with UDSAL.
• Recolor the logo to arbitrary palettes.
• Place it on noisy photos or low-contrast backgrounds.

You may use the phrase “Powered by OneLink UDSAL™” to indicate that a portal or product is built on top of this access layer.

SECTION 04 Future Vision

UDSAL as a Global Standard

From an internal OneLink prototype into an open standard that any government can adopt.

The long-term vision is for UDSAL to evolve into a global open standard for digital service access, enabling countries and organizations to plug their services into a shared schema and discovery layer while keeping full independence.

OneLink is the first implementation of this idea, starting with Saudi Arabia, Egypt, China, and global services — and later expanding to more regions and deeper integrations.
